Pushkar - The Sacred Town

Experience spiritual serenity, vibrant culture, and the world-famous camel fair in Pushkar.

About Pushkar

Pushkar is a holy town in Rajasthan known for its sacred lake and the rare Brahma Temple. With its peaceful ghats, spiritual atmosphere, and colorful bazaars, it's a popular pilgrimage site and a favorite with travelers seeking cultural and religious experiences.

Pushkar is also renowned for hosting the Pushkar Camel Fair, a spectacular event that combines livestock trading with cultural performances, competitions, and a vibrant rural carnival atmosphere.

Top 4 Tourist Spots in Pushkar

  • Pushkar Lake

    A sacred lake surrounded by 52 ghats, where pilgrims take a holy dip. The sunrise and sunset views here are especially serene.

  • Brahma Temple

    One of the few temples in the world dedicated to Lord Brahma, known for its red spire and beautiful architecture.

  • Savitri Temple

    Located atop a hill, this temple offers panoramic views of Pushkar city and the lake after a short trek or ropeway ride.

  • Pushkar Camel Fair Grounds

    The site of the annual camel and livestock fair, filled with colorful tents, decorated camels, folk music, and cultural activities.

What Food Items to Try in Pushkar

  • Malpua: A sweet pancake dessert fried in ghee and soaked in sugar syrup, a Pushkar specialty especially during festivals.
  • Dal Baati Churma: The traditional Rajasthani platter made with baked baatis, spiced dal, and sweet churma.
  • Kachori: Crispy and spicy kachoris filled with moong dal or onion, served with chutney for a savory treat.
  • Lassi: Thick, creamy, and often served in clay cups with saffron and cardamom—perfect for a hot day.
  • Street Chaat: From aloo tikki to bhel puri, Pushkar’s street food offers tangy and spicy flavors that are hard to resist.
