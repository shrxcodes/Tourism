Kolkata - The City of Joy

Experience the culture, history, and flavors of Kolkata, the City of Joy.

About Kolkata

Kolkata, the capital city of West Bengal, is a city known for its colonial architecture, rich cultural heritage, and artistic traditions. Often referred to as the "City of Joy," Kolkata is a city where history, art, and vibrant street life come together. With its iconic Howrah Bridge, Victoria Memorial, and the cultural hub of Indian cinema, the city is a place where heritage meets modernity.

Kolkata is famous for its festivals, especially Durga Puja, and is considered the cultural capital of India. It offers a rich mix of arts, literature, and theater, along with mouthwatering Bengali cuisine. Whether it's the bustling markets, the historic architecture, or the sweet treats, Kolkata is a city that has something for everyone.

Top 4 Tourist Spots in Kolkata

  • Victoria Memorial

    Victoria Memorial is an iconic white marble building and museum in the heart of Kolkata. Built in the memory of Queen Victoria, it showcases British colonial architecture and houses a collection of artifacts related to India’s history.

  • Howrah Bridge

    The Howrah Bridge, also known as Rabindra Setu, is one of the most recognizable landmarks in Kolkata. Connecting the city with Howrah, the bridge is an engineering marvel and a vital part of Kolkata's daily life.

  • Indian Museum

    The Indian Museum in Kolkata is the oldest and largest museum in India. It has an extensive collection of artifacts, ranging from archaeology and art to anthropology and natural history.

  • Kalighat Temple

    The Kalighat Temple is one of the 51 Shakti Peethas and a major pilgrimage site for Hindus. Located in the southern part of Kolkata, this temple is dedicated to Goddess Kali and is known for its spiritual significance.

What Kolkata is Famous For

  • Rosogolla: A popular Bengali sweet made of chhena (cottage cheese), soaked in sugary syrup. This sweet is synonymous with Kolkata and is a must-try.
  • Mishti Doi: A traditional Bengali dessert made from sweetened yogurt. It’s creamy, tangy, and a perfect end to any meal.
  • Kathi Roll: A street food favorite, Kathi rolls are parathas stuffed with spiced fillings such as chicken, mutton, or vegetables. It's a quick and delicious snack.
  • Shorshe Ilish: A signature Bengali dish made with hilsa fish cooked in mustard sauce, offering a distinct flavor loved by locals.
  • Chingri Malai Curry: A delicacy made with prawns cooked in a rich coconut milk-based gravy. This dish is a must-have for seafood lovers visiting Kolkata.
