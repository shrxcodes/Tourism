Varanasi - The Spiritual Capital of India

Experience the holy city of Varanasi, steeped in tradition, temples, and the Ganges River.

About Varanasi

Varanasi, also known as Kashi or Benares, is one of the oldest living cities in the world and a major cultural and religious hub in India. Situated on the banks of the Ganges River in Uttar Pradesh, it is known for its ghats, temples, classical music, and spiritual ambiance.

The city is revered in Hinduism, Buddhism, and Jainism, and draws pilgrims from across the world. It is believed that a dip in the sacred Ganges washes away sins. Varanasi is also known for its scholars, silk weaving, and cultural heritage.

Top 4 Tourist Spots in Varanasi

  • Dashashwamedh Ghat

    One of the oldest and most sacred ghats in Varanasi, famous for the Ganga Aarti ceremony held every evening with lamps, chants, and music.

  • Kashi Vishwanath Temple

    A major Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, this temple is one of the twelve Jyotirlingas and is deeply revered by devotees.

  • Sarnath

    Located just outside Varanasi, Sarnath is where Gautama Buddha gave his first sermon. It is home to ancient stupas, museums, and the Ashokan pillar.

  • Manikarnika Ghat

    This ghat is one of the oldest and most sacred cremation sites, where the eternal flame never dies. It is significant in Hindu beliefs regarding moksha (liberation).

What Food Items to Try

  • Kachori Sabzi: A popular breakfast, crispy kachoris are served with spicy and tangy aloo sabzi, often enjoyed with jalebi.
  • Banarasi Paan: A cultural icon of Varanasi, this sweet and flavorful betel leaf preparation is a must-try.
  • Malaiyo: A winter delicacy, this frothy milk dessert is flavored with saffron and cardamom and served in small earthen pots.
  • Thandai: A refreshing milk-based drink with dry fruits and spices, often associated with the festival of Holi.
  • Choora Matar: A unique Varanasi snack made from flattened rice and green peas, garnished with spices and coriander.
