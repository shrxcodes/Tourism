Hampi - City of Ruins

Explore the ancient heritage, stone temples, and serene landscapes of Hampi.

About Hampi

Hampi, located in Karnataka, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site famous for its ancient temples, grand stone monuments, and captivating ruins from the Vijayanagara Empire. This historical town is situated along the banks of the Tungabhadra River and is a paradise for history lovers and architecture enthusiasts.

With captivating landmarks like the Virupaksha Temple, Vittala Temple with its iconic stone chariot, and scenic spots like Matanga Hill, Hampi offers a unique blend of spirituality, history, and nature. The laid-back vibe and charming cafes make it a favorite among backpackers and explorers.

Top 4 Tourist Spots in Hampi

  • Virupaksha Temple

    Dedicated to Lord Shiva, Virupaksha Temple is the most prominent religious site in Hampi. It has been an active worship site since the 7th century and features intricate carvings and stunning architecture.

  • Vittala Temple

    Famous for its stone chariot and musical pillars, the Vittala Temple showcases the pinnacle of Vijayanagara architectural brilliance. It's a must-visit for art and history lovers.

  • Matanga Hill

    Ideal for sunrise and sunset views, Matanga Hill offers panoramic sights of the ruins and landscape. It's a popular trekking spot among tourists.

  • Hampi Bazaar

    Once a thriving market during the Vijayanagara reign, Hampi Bazaar is now lined with ruins, shops, and cafés. It's a great place for cultural exploration and shopping for local handicrafts.

What Food Items to Try in Hampi

  • Ragi Mudde: A nutritious ball made from finger millet flour, typically served with spicy sambar or curry — a staple in rural Karnataka.
  • South Indian Thali: A wholesome meal featuring rice, sambhar, rasam, vegetable curry, chutneys, and papad — all on a single plate.
  • Mango Lassi: A refreshing yogurt-based drink blended with ripe mangoes, ideal after a sunny walk among the ruins.
  • Banana Pancakes: A favorite among travelers, especially around Hippie Island, served with honey or chocolate.
  • Dosa & Idli: Classic South Indian breakfast items served with coconut chutney and piping hot sambar.
