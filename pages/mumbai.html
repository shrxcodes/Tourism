Mumbai - The City of Dreams

Explore the vibrant city of Mumbai, where tradition meets modernity by the Arabian Sea.

About Mumbai

Mumbai, the capital of Maharashtra, is India’s financial and entertainment hub. Formerly known as Bombay, the city is known for its fast pace, thriving film industry (Bollywood), and colonial-era architecture.

From glamorous high-rises to historical landmarks and street food stalls, Mumbai offers a unique blend of modernity and heritage that draws millions of tourists every year.

Top 4 Tourist Spots in Mumbai

  • Gateway of India

    A historic arch monument built during the British Raj, located on the waterfront in Colaba and a popular starting point for exploring the city.

  • Marine Drive

    A scenic boulevard along the Arabian Sea, famous for its sunset views and the sparkling Queen’s Necklace at night.

  • Elephanta Caves

    Ancient rock-cut temples on Elephanta Island, featuring sculptures of Lord Shiva, accessible by ferry from the Gateway of India.

  • Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Terminus

    A UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of India’s busiest railway stations, known for its Gothic architecture and historical significance.

What Food Items to Try in Mumbai

  • Vada Pav: Mumbai’s most iconic street snack—a spicy potato fritter in a bun, served with chutneys and fried green chili.
  • Pav Bhaji: A buttery mix of mashed vegetables served with soft bread rolls, perfect for any time of day.
  • Bombay Sandwich: A layered sandwich filled with vegetables, chutney, and spices, grilled to perfection.
  • Bhel Puri: A tangy and crunchy snack made from puffed rice, sev, and chutneys, commonly found on beaches like Juhu and Chowpatty.
  • Kebabs and Rolls at Mohammad Ali Road: A haven for non-veg lovers offering seekh kebabs, nalli nihari, and more during Ramadan nights.
