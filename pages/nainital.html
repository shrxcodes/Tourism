Experience the serene lakes, green hills, and colonial charm of Nainital.

About Nainital

Nainital, located in the Kumaon region of Uttarakhand, is a beautiful hill station centered around the emerald-colored Naini Lake. Surrounded by hills and dotted with colonial-era buildings, it offers a perfect escape for nature lovers and city dwellers alike.

The town is named after the Naina Devi Temple and offers a calm retreat with scenic boat rides, ropeways, and panoramic views from various hilltops. Nainital is also a hub for education and history, being home to renowned institutions like Sherwood College and the Governor’s House.

Top 4 Tourist Spots in Nainital

  • Naini Lake

    The centerpiece of Nainital, ideal for boating and evening strolls along the Mall Road.

  • Naina Devi Temple

    A sacred Hindu temple located at the northern end of the lake, dedicated to Goddess Naina Devi.

  • Tiffin Top (Dorothy’s Seat)

    A famous viewpoint offering stunning panoramic views of Nainital and the Himalayas.

  • Nainital Zoo

    A well-maintained high-altitude zoo hosting endangered species like the snow leopard and Himalayan bear.

What Food Items to Try

  • Aloo Ke Gutke: A popular Kumaoni snack made with boiled potatoes, mustard seeds, and local spices.
  • Ras: A traditional Kumaoni lentil soup served with rice, high in protein and nutrition.
  • Bhatt Ki Churkani: A tangy black soybean curry cooked with buttermilk and spices.
  • Gulgula: Sweet fried balls made with jaggery and wheat flour, served as a local dessert.
  • Bal Mithai: A brown chocolate-like fudge made from khoya and coated with sugar balls.
