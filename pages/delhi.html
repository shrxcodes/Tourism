Delhi - The Heart of India

Discover the rich culture, historic landmarks, and vibrant food scene of Delhi.

About Delhi

Delhi, the capital city of India, is a vibrant metropolis with a rich history and diverse culture. It has been the seat of many dynasties, including the Mughals and British, and is home to landmarks that reflect its historical significance, including the Red Fort, India Gate, and Jama Masjid.

Delhi is a blend of modernity and tradition. While New Delhi showcases government buildings, shopping malls, and trendy cafes, Old Delhi offers a glimpse of the Mughal era with its narrow lanes, bustling markets, and centuries-old monuments.

Top 4 Tourist Spots in Delhi

  • Red Fort

    The Red Fort is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, built by Emperor Shah Jahan in the 17th century. The fort's massive walls, beautiful gardens, and intricate architecture reflect the grandeur of the Mughal empire.

  • India Gate

    The India Gate is a war memorial dedicated to Indian soldiers who died during World War I. It stands tall as a symbol of national pride and is a popular spot for picnics and evening walks.

  • Qutub Minar

    The Qutub Minar, the tallest brick minaret in the world, is another UNESCO World Heritage Site. It was built in the 12th century and is an important example of Indo-Islamic Afghan architecture.

  • Jama Masjid

    Jama Masjid is one of the largest mosques in India and a stunning example of Mughal architecture. The mosque offers a peaceful atmosphere and beautiful views of Old Delhi.

What Delhi is Famous For

  • Chaat: Delhi is known for its variety of chaats, including Pani Puri, Aloo Tikki, and Papri Chaat. These street snacks are tangy, spicy, and absolutely delicious.
  • Butter Chicken: Delhi is the birthplace of Butter Chicken, a creamy and flavorful dish made with marinated chicken in a rich tomato-based gravy. It's a must-try for food lovers.
  • Kebabs: The city is known for its mouthwatering kebabs, especially from the famous Karim's restaurant. You can try the Seekh Kebabs, Galouti Kebabs, and Tandoori Kebabs.
  • Paratha: Delhi is famous for its stuffed parathas. The Paratha Wali Gali in Chandni Chowk is a famous spot where you can try a variety of parathas, from classic to innovative.
  • Lassi: The rich and creamy lassi, available in many flavors, is a refreshing drink enjoyed by locals. The lassi from the famous Moolchand Paratha is a must-try.
