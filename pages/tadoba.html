Tadoba Andhari National Park

Discover the beauty of Tadoba Andhari, the land of tigers, with a unique blend of rich wildlife and pristine nature.

About Tadoba Andhari

Tadoba Andhari National Park, located in Maharashtra, India, is one of the oldest and largest national parks in the state. Known as "The Land of Tigers," Tadoba is renowned for its high tiger density and exceptional wildlife experiences. The park spans over 625 square kilometers and is home to a rich diversity of flora and fauna, including tigers, leopards, wild dogs, sloth bears, and a wide variety of bird species.

Established in 1955, Tadoba was initially a sanctuary before being upgraded to a national park in 1995. The park's diverse habitats, ranging from dense forests to open grasslands, make it an ideal environment for wildlife conservation. Tadoba is part of the larger Tadoba-Andhari Tiger Reserve, which plays a crucial role in protecting the endangered Bengal tiger population.

Wildlife to Spot

  • Bengal Tiger

    Tadoba is home to one of the highest densities of Bengal tigers in India. Spotting these majestic predators is the highlight of a visit to the park.

  • Leopard

    Leopards, though elusive, are frequently spotted in Tadoba, especially in the park's dense forested areas.

  • Wild Dog (Dhole)

    The wild dog, also known as the dhole, is a social carnivore that hunts in packs and is often seen in the park.

  • Sloth Bear

    The sloth bear, known for its nocturnal nature, can be found in the park, particularly in the more secluded regions.

  • Birds

    Tadoba is a birdwatcher's paradise, with over 200 species of birds, including migratory species that visit the park during the winter months.

Safari Experiences

  • Jeep Safari

    The jeep safari is the best way to explore the park, providing an opportunity to see tigers, leopards, and other wildlife in their natural habitat.

  • Canter Safari

    The canter safari is a more economical option for large groups, offering an equally thrilling wildlife experience in the park.

  • Bird Watching Tours

    With over 200 bird species, bird watching tours in Tadoba are a must for nature lovers. These tours allow visitors to spot both resident and migratory birds.

  • Photography Tours

    Photography tours are tailored for wildlife photographers who want to capture the stunning beauty of Tadoba's landscapes and wildlife in perfect light.
