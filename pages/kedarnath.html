Kedarnath - The Abode of Lord Shiva

Discover the spiritual sanctity of Kedarnath, one of the Char Dham pilgrimage sites in Uttarakhand.

About Kedarnath

Kedarnath is a sacred town nestled in the Garhwal Himalayas in Uttarakhand, India, and is one of the Char Dham pilgrimage sites. It is primarily known for the Kedarnath Temple, dedicated to Lord Shiva, and is visited by thousands of devotees every year. The town is set at an altitude of 3,580 meters and is surrounded by majestic snow-capped mountains, offering breathtaking views to visitors.

Kedarnath's serenity and spiritual ambiance make it a perfect retreat for those seeking peace and divine blessings. The trek to Kedarnath Temple from Gaurikund is an arduous but rewarding journey that offers incredible natural beauty along the way.

Top 4 Tourist Spots in Kedarnath

  • Kedarnath Temple

    The Kedarnath Temple is the primary attraction in the region, dedicated to Lord Shiva. This 1,000-year-old temple is perched on the banks of the Mandakini River and is a significant pilgrimage site for Hindus. The temple is accessible via a steep trek, and the views from the temple are mesmerizing.

  • Gaurikund

    Gaurikund is the base camp for the Kedarnath trek. It is known for its hot springs, where pilgrims take a dip before starting their journey to the Kedarnath Temple. The serene and peaceful environment makes it a popular spot for tourists to relax and unwind.

  • Chorabari Tal

    Chorabari Tal, also known as Gandhi Sarovar, is a pristine lake situated 3 km from Kedarnath. The lake is surrounded by towering mountains and offers a tranquil setting, perfect for a short trek and spiritual reflection.

  • Shankaracharya Samadhi

    The Shankaracharya Samadhi is a revered site located near the Kedarnath Temple. It is believed to be the place where the great philosopher and saint, Adi Shankaracharya, meditated and attained salvation. The site offers panoramic views of the surrounding mountains.

What Kedarnath is Famous For

  • Prasad (Holy Offering): Kedarnath offers prasad to the devotees, which is considered sacred. It consists of a mixture of dry fruits, jaggery, and other offerings, which are consumed by pilgrims as a sign of blessings from Lord Shiva.
  • Rajma Chawal: A hearty dish made with kidney beans (rajma) cooked in a spicy gravy and served with steamed rice. It’s a comforting meal for trekkers after a long day of hiking.
  • Aloo Ke Parathe: A traditional Indian bread stuffed with spiced mashed potatoes. It’s a popular dish for breakfast, especially among pilgrims on their way to the temple.
  • Garhwali Thali: A traditional thali (platter) from the Garhwal region, consisting of rice, dal, vegetables, chapati, and pickles. It’s a nutritious and flavorful meal commonly served to tourists.
  • Chai (Tea): The tea served in Kedarnath, especially near the trekking routes, is famous for its warmth and the much-needed refreshment it provides to pilgrims trekking to the temple.
