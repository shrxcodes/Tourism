Gir National Park

Explore the wilderness and diverse wildlife of Gir, home to the majestic Asiatic Lion.

About Gir National Park

Gir National Park, located in the western state of Gujarat, India, is the last natural habitat of the endangered Asiatic Lion. The park spans over 1,400 square kilometers of forest and grassland and is one of the most important wildlife sanctuaries in India. It is home to a variety of wildlife, including leopards, crocodiles, and several species of birds, making it a top destination for wildlife enthusiasts.

Established in 1965, Gir has become a symbol of successful wildlife conservation, with the Asiatic Lion population increasing significantly over the years. The park is a key example of how a species can be saved from extinction through dedicated conservation efforts.

Wildlife to Spot

  • Asiatic Lion

    The Asiatic Lion is the pride of Gir, and spotting one during a safari is an unforgettable experience. The park is home to over 500 lions, making it the only place in the world where they can be seen in the wild.

  • Leopard

    Leopards are frequently seen in Gir, especially in the park's dense forests, where they lurk in the shadows, adding an element of mystery to the wildlife experience.

  • Indian Crocodile

    Gir is home to a significant population of Indian Crocodiles, particularly along the rivers and lakes within the park. These reptiles are often seen basking in the sun.

  • Spotted Deer

    Spotted Deer, also known as Chital, are commonly seen grazing in the open grasslands of the park and are often spotted in herds.

  • Birds

    Gir is home to over 300 species of birds, including both resident and migratory species, making it a paradise for birdwatchers.

Safari Experiences

  • Jeep Safari

    The Jeep Safari is the best way to explore the diverse habitats of Gir and get a chance to spot the Asiatic Lion, along with other wildlife like leopards and crocodiles.

  • Lion Safari

    A special safari designed specifically to increase the chances of spotting the Asiatic Lions in their natural habitat, this safari provides an incredible opportunity to observe these majestic creatures.
