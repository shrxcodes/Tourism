Bikaner - The Camel City

Experience the royal heritage, camel safaris, and spicy cuisine of the desert town of Bikaner.

About Bikaner

Bikaner, located in the northwest of Rajasthan, is a city known for its rich cultural heritage, majestic forts, and camel breeding. The city is surrounded by the Thar Desert and showcases grand palaces, colorful markets, and traditional Rajasthani hospitality.

Bikaner is also famous for hosting the annual Camel Festival, which celebrates the importance of camels in desert life with parades, races, and cultural performances.

Top 4 Tourist Spots in Bikaner

  • Junagarh Fort

    A massive and well-preserved fort that houses palaces, courtyards, and temples with intricate architecture and historic artifacts.

  • Karni Mata Temple (Rat Temple)

    A unique temple in Deshnok, famous for its large population of sacred rats that roam freely and are considered holy.

  • Lalgarh Palace

    An architectural marvel built in red sandstone, blending Rajput, Mughal, and European styles. Parts of the palace are now a heritage hotel and museum.

  • National Research Centre on Camel

    Learn about different camel breeds, enjoy camel rides, and try camel milk ice cream at this unique research facility.

What Food Items to Try in Bikaner

  • Bikaneri Bhujia: A world-famous spicy snack made from moth beans, gram flour, and a blend of Rajasthani spices.
  • Kachori: Crisp fried pastries filled with spicy lentils or onions, often served with tangy chutney.
