About Kumarakom

Kumarakom, located in the Kottayam district of Kerala, is a tranquil village famous for its backwaters, houseboats, and lush landscapes. Situated along the shores of Vembanad Lake, it is a haven for nature lovers, bird watchers, and those seeking a peaceful retreat from the hustle and bustle of everyday life.

The village is known for its beautiful landscapes, rich biodiversity, and serene backwater cruises. Kumarakom is also home to the famous Kumarakom Bird Sanctuary, which attracts bird watchers from across the globe.

Top 4 Tourist Spots in Kumarakom

  • Kumarakom Backwaters

    The backwaters of Kumarakom offer peaceful houseboat cruises through the tranquil waters, surrounded by lush coconut groves and rustic village life.

  • Kumarakom Bird Sanctuary

    Located along the Vembanad Lake, this sanctuary is home to a variety of migratory birds, making it a popular spot for bird watchers.

  • Vembanad Lake

    The largest lake in Kerala, Vembanad Lake offers boating, fishing, and scenic views of the surrounding landscapes. It is also known for the annual Nehru Trophy Boat Race.

  • Aruvikkuzhi Waterfall

    A beautiful waterfall located near Kumarakom, ideal for trekking and enjoying the refreshing sights and sounds of nature.

What Food Items to Try

  • Karimeen Pollichathu: A traditional Kerala delicacy made with pearl spot fish, marinated with spices and wrapped in banana leaves, then grilled.
  • Appam with Stew: Soft rice pancakes served with a coconut-based vegetable stew, a quintessential Kerala dish.
  • Puttu and Kadala Curry: Steamed rice cakes served with a spicy black chickpea curry, a popular breakfast dish in Kerala.
  • Fish Curry: A tangy and spicy curry made with fresh fish and local Kerala spices, best enjoyed with rice.
  • Banana Chips: Fried crispy banana chips, a popular snack in Kerala, often enjoyed with meals or as a standalone treat.
