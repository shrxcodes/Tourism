Chilika Lake - India’s Largest Brackish Water Lagoon

Explore the beauty and biodiversity of Chilika Lake, one of India’s most important ecosystems.

About Chilika Lake

Chilika Lake, located on the eastern coast of India in the state of Odisha, is the largest brackish water lagoon in Asia and the second largest in the world. The lake spans over 1,100 square kilometers and is a haven for migratory birds, especially during the winter months.

Chilika is not just a natural beauty; it is also an important ecosystem that supports a variety of aquatic species, including fish, crabs, and prawns. The lake is known for its rich biodiversity, scenic landscapes, and vibrant fishing communities.

Top 4 Tourist Spots in Chilika Lake

  • Satapada

    Satapada is famous for its Dolphin Sanctuary. It is one of the best places to spot the endangered Irrawaddy dolphins, which reside in the lagoon.

  • Kalijai Island

    Kalijai Island is a popular pilgrimage site with a temple dedicated to Goddess Kalijai, set amidst the scenic beauty of the lake.

  • Bird Sanctuary at Nalabana

    This sanctuary is home to thousands of migratory birds, making it a paradise for bird watchers, especially during the winter season.

  • Rajhans Island

    Rajhans Island offers a peaceful escape, surrounded by stunning natural beauty and offering opportunities for water activities like boating and fishing.

What Food Items to Try

  • Chilika Fish Curry: A special dish made with fresh fish from the lake, cooked in a spicy, tangy gravy that is a local favorite.
  • Chhena Poda: A traditional Odisha dessert made from fresh cheese, sugar, and cardamom, baked until golden brown.
  • Fried Prawn Pakoda: A delicious crispy snack made from prawns, seasoned with spices and deep-fried to perfection.
  • Odisha Dalma: A hearty lentil and vegetable dish, often served with rice, representing the culinary flavors of Odisha.
  • Chilika Crab Curry: A flavorful curry made with fresh crabs from Chilika Lake, cooked with aromatic spices and herbs.
