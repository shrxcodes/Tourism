Sundarbans National Park

Discover the beauty and biodiversity of Sundarbans, a UNESCO World Heritage Site and home to the Royal Bengal Tiger.

About Sundarbans

Sundarbans National Park, located in the delta region of the Ganges, Brahmaputra, and Meghna rivers, is a vast mangrove forest in West Bengal, India. A UNESCO World Heritage Site, Sundarbans is known for its unique ecosystem, which includes saltwater crocodiles, tigers, and diverse species of birds and marine life. The park is most famous for being the home of the Royal Bengal Tiger, often spotted lurking in the dense mangrove forests.

The park's landscape, interspersed with tidal rivers, mudflats, and islands, offers a rich biodiversity that has made it one of the most important wetland ecosystems in the world. The Sundarbans play a crucial role in protecting the coastal regions from storms and tides, making it vital to both wildlife and human life in the area.

Wildlife to Spot

  • Royal Bengal Tiger

    The Royal Bengal Tiger is the star attraction of the Sundarbans. It is one of the most elusive and endangered big cats, and seeing one in the wild is an extraordinary experience.

  • Sundarbans Saltwater Crocodile

    The Sundarbans is home to the largest population of saltwater crocodiles, which inhabit the tidal rivers and coastal areas of the park.

  • Indian Cobra

    The Sundarbans are also home to a variety of reptiles, including the Indian Cobra, which is a venomous species found in the forest.

  • Spotted Deer

    Commonly seen in the park, the Spotted Deer are often found grazing in the forests and are a food source for the tigers.

Safari Experiences

  • Boat Safari

    The best way to explore the dense mangroves and spot wildlife, including the elusive Bengal Tiger, is by boat safari. Visitors can also see saltwater crocodiles and a variety of birds.

  • Nature Walks

    Accompanied by experienced guides, visitors can embark on nature walks to discover the flora and fauna of the Sundarbans, though sightings of tigers may be rare due to the dense vegetation.

  • Bird Watching Tours

    These tours allow bird enthusiasts to observe the various species that inhabit the park, including migratory birds during the winter months.

  • Photography Tours

    For photographers, Sundarbans offers excellent opportunities to capture the incredible wildlife, lush landscapes, and unique biodiversity of the park.
