Ahmedabad - The City of Pols

Explore the vibrant culture, architecture, and rich history of Ahmedabad, Gujarat's largest city.

About Ahmedabad

Ahmedabad, located in the state of Gujarat, is known for its rich cultural heritage, historical landmarks, and thriving textile industry. The city is a vibrant mix of old-world charm with its ancient havelis and modern attractions. Known as the "Manchester of the East," it is a significant center for commerce and industry in India.

Famous for its beautiful stepwells, traditional pol houses, and the Sabarmati Riverfront, Ahmedabad is a must-visit destination for history buffs, culture lovers, and foodies alike.

Top 4 Tourist Spots in Ahmedabad

  • Sabarmati Ashram

    A historical site where Mahatma Gandhi lived for a period of time, Sabarmati Ashram offers insight into his life and teachings. The ashram is located on the banks of the Sabarmati River.

  • Adalaj Stepwell

    A stunning five-story stepwell known for its intricate carvings and beautiful architecture, Adalaj Stepwell offers a glimpse into the region's history and water management systems.

  • Sarkhej Roza

    A historical complex with tombs, mosques, and a beautiful pond, Sarkhej Roza is known for its Islamic architecture and peaceful ambiance.

  • Manek Chowk

    A bustling market square that is famous for its street food, including the famous Gujarati dishes like pav bhaji, dabeli, and chaat.
