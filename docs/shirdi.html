Shirdi - The Abode of Sai Baba

Experience the spirituality and tranquility of Shirdi, the holy town of Sai Baba.

About Shirdi

Shirdi, a small town in Maharashtra, India, is famous for being the home of the revered spiritual leader Sai Baba. It is a place where millions of devotees from all over the world visit to seek blessings and solace. Shirdi is renowned for the Sai Baba Temple, which attracts pilgrims and tourists alike.

The town offers a peaceful, spiritual ambiance with numerous temples and sacred sites dedicated to Sai Baba. Apart from its religious significance, Shirdi's charm lies in its simplicity and deep connection to Sai Baba's teachings of selfless service, compassion, and spirituality.

Top 4 Tourist Spots in Shirdi

  • Sai Baba Temple

    The Sai Baba Temple in Shirdi is the main attraction, where millions of devotees visit to pay their respects to Sai Baba. The temple is a beautiful blend of traditional and modern architecture, housing the samadhi (resting place) of Sai Baba.

  • Dwarkamai

    Dwarkamai is a mosque where Sai Baba spent much of his time. It is a significant spiritual site for Sai Baba's followers. The holy fire (dhuni) that Sai Baba lit continues to burn here, symbolizing purity and selfless love.

  • Shani Shingnapur

    Shani Shingnapur is a famous temple dedicated to Lord Shani, located near Shirdi. It is known for the belief that the deity does not need a roof over the shrine, as it is considered to be self-protected by divine powers.

  • Chavadi

    Chavadi is a small building where Sai Baba used to sleep alternately with Dwarkamai. The site has a peaceful vibe, with a simple yet divine atmosphere that attracts pilgrims looking for a deeper spiritual experience.

What Shirdi is Famous For

  • Prasad (Holy Offering): The prasad served at Sai Baba Temple is highly revered. It consists of sweet offerings like laddoos and chana (chickpeas), which are distributed to pilgrims as a token of blessings.
  • Shirdi Puran Poli: A delicious sweet flatbread stuffed with jaggery and lentils. This traditional dish is popular in Shirdi and is often enjoyed by devotees.
  • Pithla Bhakri: A traditional Maharashtrian dish, Pithla Bhakri consists of spicy gram flour (pithla) served with thick unleavened bread (bhakri). It is a comfort food enjoyed by locals and tourists alike.
