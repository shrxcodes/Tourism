Jaipur - The Pink City

Explore the vibrant culture, stunning architecture, and royal heritage of Jaipur.

About Jaipur

Jaipur, the capital city of Rajasthan, India, is known as the "Pink City" due to its distinctive pink-colored architecture. Founded by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II in 1727, Jaipur is renowned for its rich history, vibrant culture, and magnificent forts and palaces. The city features an impressive blend of Mughal and traditional Rajasthani architecture, making it a visual delight for visitors.

Among its famous attractions are the Hawa Mahal, Amber Fort, and the City Palace. Jaipur is also known for its bustling markets offering vibrant textiles, jewelry, and handicrafts. The city hosts the grand Jaipur Literature Festival and the famous Elephant Festival, making it a cultural hub for both locals and tourists alike. Jaipur is also famous for its delicious Rajasthani cuisine.

Top 4 Tourist Spots in Jaipur

  • Amber Fort

    A majestic hilltop fort known for its artistic Hindu-style elements and beautiful views.

  • Hawa Mahal

    Iconic pink sandstone palace with 953 small windows designed for royal women to observe festivals.

  • City Palace

    Historic palace complex that blends Mughal and Rajasthani architecture, housing museums and courtyards.

  • Jantar Mantar

    UNESCO World Heritage Site featuring a collection of astronomical instruments built in the 18th century.

What Food Items to Try

  • Dal Baati Churma: A traditional Rajasthani dish consisting of spicy dal, baked wheat balls, and sweet churma.
  • Ghevar: A festive sweet soaked in syrup, garnished with dry fruits and silver leaf.
  • Laal Maas: A fiery red mutton curry rich in traditional spices and red chilies.
  • Kachori: Crispy deep-fried snacks filled with spicy lentils or onions, commonly eaten with chutney.
  • Jalebi: A sugary, deep-fried dessert with a crisp texture and syrupy sweetness.
