About Shimla

Shimla, the capital of Himachal Pradesh, is one of India's most popular hill stations. Nestled in the foothills of the Himalayas, Shimla is known for its colonial architecture, cool climate, and scenic mountain views.

Once the summer capital of British India, Shimla still carries the charm of the bygone era. Its bustling Mall Road, historic buildings, and pine-covered hills make it a favorite destination for tourists seeking relaxation and nature.

Top 4 Tourist Spots in Shimla

  • The Ridge

    A large open space in the heart of the town offering panoramic views of the surrounding mountains.

  • Jakhoo Temple

    Dedicated to Lord Hanuman, this temple is situated on the highest peak of Shimla and offers stunning views.

  • Kufri

    A small hill station near Shimla, famous for skiing, trekking, and horse riding during winter.

  • Christ Church

    One of the oldest churches in North India, featuring neo-Gothic architecture and beautiful stained glass windows.

What Food Items to Try

  • Chana Madra: A Himachali delicacy made of chickpeas in a thick yogurt-based gravy.
  • Sidu: A local bread made from wheat flour, stuffed with nuts or lentils and steamed.
  • Thukpa: A warm noodle soup with vegetables or meat, popular in colder regions of Shimla.
  • Tudkiya Bhat: A spicy rice dish made with lentils, potatoes, and yogurt.
  • Babru: A Himachali version of kachori, stuffed with black gram paste and deep fried.
