Jodhpur - The Blue City

Discover the charm of Rajasthan's Blue City, with its grand forts, vivid streets, and cultural richness.

About Jodhpur

Jodhpur, often called the Blue City, is known for its striking blue-painted houses that surround the mighty Mehrangarh Fort. Situated at the edge of the Thar Desert, it offers a majestic blend of history, architecture, and cultural heritage.

The city boasts of palaces, forts, temples, traditional bazaars, and delicious Marwari cuisine, making it a must-visit destination for heritage and culture enthusiasts.

Top 4 Tourist Spots in Jodhpur

  • Mehrangarh Fort

    One of the largest forts in India, offering panoramic views of the blue city and featuring palaces, galleries, and courtyards.

  • Jaswant Thada

    A stunning white marble cenotaph built in memory of Maharaja Jaswant Singh II, surrounded by peaceful gardens and a lake.

  • Umaid Bhawan Palace

    A magnificent heritage palace, part of which functions as a luxury hotel and museum showcasing royal artifacts.

  • Clock Tower and Sardar Market

    A vibrant market near the clock tower offering spices, handicrafts, textiles, and local street food.
