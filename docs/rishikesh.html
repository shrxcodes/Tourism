Rishikesh - The Yoga Capital of the World

Discover spirituality, adventure, and the beauty of nature in Rishikesh.

About Rishikesh

Rishikesh, located in the foothills of the Himalayas, is a small yet spiritual city in Uttarakhand, India. It is known as the "Yoga Capital of the World" and draws millions of tourists seeking peace, meditation, and yoga. The city is surrounded by lush green forests, sacred rivers, and stunning mountain views, making it the perfect place for spiritual rejuvenation and adventure activities.

Rishikesh is also famous for its iconic suspension bridge, Lakshman Jhula, the holy Ganges River, and vibrant ashrams that offer yoga and meditation classes. The city hosts the International Yoga Festival every year and has become a hub for spiritual seekers and yoga enthusiasts from around the globe.

Top 4 Tourist Spots in Rishikesh

  • Lakshman Jhula

    Lakshman Jhula is a famous suspension bridge over the Ganges, which offers stunning views of the river and surrounding hills. It holds immense religious significance and is one of Rishikesh's most iconic landmarks.

  • Triveni Ghat

    Triveni Ghat is a sacred ghat where devotees gather to take a holy dip in the Ganges. The evening Ganga Aarti here is a mesmerizing sight, attracting visitors for both its spiritual and cultural significance.

  • Neelkanth Mahadev Temple

    Situated at a height of 1,330 meters, the Neelkanth Mahadev Temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva. It is a popular pilgrimage site and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding forests and mountains.

  • Rajaji National Park

    Located just outside Rishikesh, Rajaji National Park is a sanctuary for wildlife enthusiasts. The park is home to elephants, tigers, leopards, and various bird species, making it a great destination for safaris and nature walks.

  • Beatles Ashram

    The Beatles Ashram, also known as Chaurasi Kutia, is an ashram made famous by the Beatles, who visited Rishikesh in the 1960s for spiritual learning. Today, it remains a popular spot for meditation and exploring the ruins of the ashram.

What Rishikesh is Famous For

  • Chole Bhature: A popular North Indian dish, Chole Bhature consists of spicy chickpeas served with deep-fried bread (bhature). It’s a favorite among locals and tourists.
  • Alu Puri: A tasty street food combination of deep-fried puris served with a spicy potato curry. It is commonly enjoyed as a breakfast or snack.
  • Rishikesh Lassi: A refreshing yogurt-based drink that is available in a variety of flavors, from sweet to salty. It’s a must-try when visiting Rishikesh.
  • Ayurvedic Tea: Known for its health benefits, Ayurvedic tea made with herbs and spices is a common drink in Rishikesh. Many cafes and ashrams offer it as part of their wellness offerings.
  • Chaas (Buttermilk): A cooling drink made from yogurt and spices, Chaas is perfect for the hot weather in Rishikesh and is commonly served as a refreshing drink.
