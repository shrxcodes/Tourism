Loktak Lake - The Floating Lake of Manipur

Experience the serenity of Loktak Lake, famous for its floating islands and scenic beauty.

About Loktak Lake

Loktak Lake, located in the Indian state of Manipur, is the largest freshwater lake in northeastern India. The lake is unique for its "phumdis"â€”floating islands of vegetation and soil that float on its surface, which is an ecological marvel.

The lake is also home to diverse wildlife and is an important source of livelihood for the local fishing communities. Loktak Lake is also a hub for birdwatching, with many migratory birds visiting during the winter months. The surrounding hills and lush greenery add to the lake's tranquil beauty.

Top 4 Tourist Spots in Loktak Lake

  • Keibul Lamjao National Park

    This national park is located on the floating phumdis of Loktak Lake and is home to the endangered Sangai deer, found only in this region.

  • Sendra Island

    Sendra Island offers a panoramic view of the entire Loktak Lake, making it one of the best spots to observe the lake's floating islands and surrounding landscapes.

  • Thanga

    Thanga is a small fishing village located near Loktak Lake. It offers a chance to experience local culture and traditions, and enjoy boat rides on the lake.

  • Loktak Lake Viewpoint

    The viewpoint provides an excellent spot to admire the beauty of Loktak Lake and capture its mesmerizing scenery, especially at sunrise and sunset.

What Food Items to Try

  • Ngari: A fermented fish dish that is commonly used in Manipur cuisine, offering a unique, tangy flavor.
  • Chamthong: A light and flavorful vegetable stew, often served with rice, a staple in Manipuri cuisine.
  • Eromba: A spicy mash made with vegetables and fermented fish, typically served with rice.
  • Shingju: A Manipur salad made with leafy vegetables, chutney, and sometimes fish, offering a refreshing taste.
  • Kangsoi: A popular Manipuri soup made with vegetables and fish, flavored with traditional spices and herbs.
