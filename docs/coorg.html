Coorg - The Scotland of India

Discover the lush greenery, coffee plantations, and misty hills of Coorg in Karnataka.

About Coorg

Coorg, also known as Kodagu, is a picturesque hill station in Karnataka famous for its coffee estates, rolling hills, and serene climate. Often referred to as the "Scotland of India", Coorg attracts nature lovers, honeymooners, and adventure seekers.

The region is home to scenic waterfalls, dense forests, and rich Kodava culture. With pleasant weather year-round, Coorg is ideal for trekking, river rafting, and enjoying peaceful retreats amid nature.

Top 4 Tourist Spots in Coorg

  • Abbey Falls

    A stunning waterfall located amidst coffee plantations, offering a refreshing and scenic view.

  • Dubare Elephant Camp

    A unique experience where visitors can bathe, feed, and interact with elephants by the river.

  • Raja’s Seat

    A popular sunset point offering panoramic views of the valleys and hills, once favored by kings.

  • Talakaveri

    The origin of River Kaveri, located on Brahmagiri hill, surrounded by beautiful landscapes and temples.

What Food Items to Try

  • Pandi Curry: A rich and spicy pork curry made with Coorg’s special masala and vinegar.
  • Akki Roti: A rice flour flatbread commonly served with spicy curries and chutneys.
  • Bamboo Shoot Curry: Traditional Kodava dish made with seasonal bamboo shoots and spices.
  • Noolputtu: Coorg-style rice noodles typically paired with curries or stews.
  • Koli Curry: Spicy chicken curry infused with local flavors, often served with rice or roti.
