Agra - City of the Taj Mahal

Discover the charm of Agra with its rich history, iconic landmarks, and mouth-watering cuisine.

About Agra

Agra, located on the banks of the Yamuna River in Uttar Pradesh, is best known for the magnificent Taj Mahal—one of the Seven Wonders of the World. The city was a major center of the Mughal Empire and is filled with historical treasures and architectural marvels.

Besides the Taj Mahal, Agra is home to other UNESCO World Heritage Sites like Agra Fort and Fatehpur Sikri. It also offers a lively atmosphere, local bazaars, and street food that reflects its cultural richness.

Top 4 Tourist Spots in Agra

  • Taj Mahal

    One of the most iconic monuments in the world, the Taj Mahal is a white marble mausoleum built by Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal.

  • Agra Fort

    A massive red sandstone fort that served as the main residence of the Mughal emperors. The fort contains palaces, audience halls, and mosques.

  • Fatehpur Sikri

    A historic city built by Akbar the Great, known for its stunning architecture including Buland Darwaza and the Tomb of Salim Chishti.

  • Mehtab Bagh

    A riverside garden complex that offers the best views of the Taj Mahal, especially at sunset.

What Food Items to Try

  • Petha: A soft and translucent sweet made from ash gourd, flavored with saffron, cardamom, and rose water. Available in many variants like dry, kesar, and chocolate.
  • Bedai & Aloo Sabzi: A popular breakfast dish, Bedai is a fried puffy bread served with spicy potato curry and chutney.
  • Mughlai Cuisine: Dishes like Mutton Korma, Butter Chicken, and Biryani reflect Agra’s Mughal legacy and are rich in flavor and spices.
  • Dalmoth: A spicy and crunchy dry snack made with fried lentils and nuts—great as a takeaway item.
  • Tandoori Chicken: Spicy marinated chicken grilled in a clay oven, served with mint chutney and onions.
