Kollam - Gateway to the Backwaters of Kerala

Discover the serenity of Kollam, a beautiful coastal town offering picturesque backwaters, beaches, and vibrant culture.

About Kollam

Kollam, located on the southern coast of Kerala, is a charming city known for its scenic backwaters, beautiful beaches, and rich history. The city is a major trading hub and an important center for the coir industry, making it a unique blend of natural beauty and vibrant local culture.

The town is situated along the shores of the Ashtamudi Lake, offering a serene atmosphere perfect for houseboat cruises and nature walks. Kollam also offers a peaceful retreat for those looking to experience Kerala's traditional lifestyle, delicious seafood, and vibrant festivals.

Top 4 Tourist Spots in Kollam

  • Ashtamudi Lake

    Known for its serene backwaters, Ashtamudi Lake offers an unforgettable houseboat experience with views of lush landscapes and peaceful waters.

  • Munroe Island

    A unique island where the backwaters meet the Arabian Sea, known for its tranquil surroundings and local village life.

  • Kollam Beach

    A beautiful beach perfect for a leisurely walk or enjoying the cool breeze. It’s also ideal for sunset views and photography.

  • Sri Chitra Art Gallery

    A gallery showcasing Kerala’s rich culture and traditional art, with an impressive collection of ancient paintings and sculptures.

What Food Items to Try

  • Karimeen Pollichathu: A traditional Kerala delicacy made with pearl spot fish, marinated with spices, wrapped in banana leaves, and grilled to perfection.
  • Puttu and Kadala Curry: Steamed rice cakes served with a spicy black chickpea curry, a popular and hearty breakfast dish in Kerala.
  • Seafood: Kollam, being a coastal town, offers a wide variety of fresh seafood, including shrimp, crab, and fish, served in traditional Kerala style with spices.
  • Banana Chips: Crispy and crunchy banana chips, a common snack in Kerala, often paired with a cup of tea.
  • Vishu Kanji: A traditional dish made from rice, coconut milk, and spices, enjoyed during Kerala’s festival of Vishu.
