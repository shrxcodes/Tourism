Ooty - Queen of Hill Stations

Experience the tranquil beauty, cool climate, and green slopes of Ooty in Tamil Nadu.

About Ooty

Ooty, officially known as Udhagamandalam, is a serene hill station nestled in the Nilgiri Hills of Tamil Nadu. Often called the “Queen of Hill Stations,” Ooty is famous for its pleasant weather, scenic beauty, and tea gardens. The British developed it as a summer retreat, and its charm continues to draw travelers today.

From toy train rides to boat rides and botanical gardens, Ooty offers a perfect mix of relaxation and nature. The town is dotted with colonial-style architecture and surrounded by eucalyptus forests, waterfalls, and misty valleys.

Top 4 Tourist Spots in Ooty

  • Ooty Lake

    A popular boating spot surrounded by eucalyptus trees, offering paddleboats and rowboats.

  • Botanical Garden

    Sprawling garden with exotic plants and flowers, ideal for nature lovers and picnickers.

  • Doddabetta Peak

    The highest point in the Nilgiris, providing panoramic views of the surrounding hills.

  • Nilgiri Mountain Railway

    A UNESCO heritage toy train ride offering breathtaking views of tunnels, bridges, and tea estates.

What Food Items to Try

  • Ooty Varkey: A crispy baked snack made from wheat flour, semolina, and ghee—unique to Ooty.
  • Homemade Chocolates: Delicious, creamy chocolates available in various flavors across local shops.
  • South Indian Thali: A full traditional meal with rice, sambar, rasam, poriyal, and curd.
  • Nilgiri Tea: Freshly brewed tea from local plantations, often served spiced or flavored.
  • Biryani: Spicy and flavorful rice dish available in both vegetarian and non-vegetarian versions.
