Kanha National Park

Discover the beauty of Kanha, a stunning tiger reserve in Madhya Pradesh, India, home to rich biodiversity.

About Kanha

Kanha National Park, located in Madhya Pradesh, India, is one of the largest and most popular wildlife parks in India. Spanning across 940 square kilometers, the park is a part of the Kanha Tiger Reserve and is renowned for its efforts in the conservation of the Royal Bengal Tiger. Kanha offers diverse landscapes, including dense forests, open meadows, and waterholes, making it an ideal habitat for various species of flora and fauna.

Established in 1955, the park is home to a large population of tigers, leopards, wild dogs, and other animals. Its dense Sal and bamboo forests provide shelter for a variety of wildlife, while the meadows and grasslands attract herbivores like the Indian Gazelle and Barasingha (Swamp Deer), a species endemic to the park.

Wildlife to Spot

  • Royal Bengal Tiger

    The Royal Bengal Tiger is the primary attraction of Kanha National Park, and the park is famous for its successful tiger conservation efforts.

  • Leopard

    Leopards, though elusive, are present in the park, and sightings are considered a rare and thrilling experience for visitors.

  • Indian Wild Dog (Dhole)

    These social animals, known for their hunting prowess, are a common sight in Kanha's forests, often spotted in packs.

  • Barasingha (Swamp Deer)

    One of the park's success stories, the endangered Barasingha thrives here due to the park's conservation initiatives, particularly in the central grasslands.

  • Birds

    With over 300 species of birds, Kanha is a bird-watching paradise. Visitors can spot various species including the Painted Stork, Indian Peafowl, and Black-shouldered Kite.

Safari Experiences

  • Jeep Safari

    The jeep safari is the best way to explore Kanha, offering visitors the chance to spot tigers, leopards, and other wildlife in their natural habitat.

  • Elephant Safari

    Elephant safaris provide a unique perspective of the park, allowing tourists to get close to the park's wildlife, particularly tigers and other large mammals.

  • Photography Tours

    Specialized photography tours are available for those looking to capture the stunning wildlife and landscapes of Kanha on camera.

  • Nature Walks

    Guided nature walks allow tourists to experience the park from a different perspective, focusing on the smaller flora and fauna that might be overlooked during a safari.
