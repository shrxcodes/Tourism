Barmer - Desert Gem of Rajasthan

Experience the rustic charm, folk traditions, and artistic brilliance of Barmer in the Thar Desert.

About Barmer

Barmer, located in western Rajasthan, is known for its vibrant culture, traditional handicrafts, and scenic desert landscapes. It is a treasure trove of ancient temples, colorful festivals, and desert dunes.

The town comes alive during the Barmer Festival with folk music, camel rides, and craft exhibitions. It's also famous for wood carving, embroidery, and pottery that reflect its cultural richness.

Top 4 Tourist Spots in Barmer

  • Kiradu Temples

    A group of 11th-century temples known for their intricate sandstone carvings and historical significance.

  • Barmer Fort

    A historic fort offering views of the desert city, with beautiful temples within its complex.

  • Mahabar Sand Dunes

    Located close to town, these dunes provide an excellent location for desert safaris and sunset views.

  • Jasol

    An ancient town near Barmer known for its Rajput architecture and the Jasol Fort and temples.

What Food Items to Try in Barmer

  • Ker Sangri: A traditional Rajasthani dish made with desert beans and dried berries cooked in spices and oil.
  • Bajre ki Roti: Millet flatbread served with ghee and garlic chutney—staple of desert cuisine.
  • Gatte ki Sabzi: Gram flour dumplings in a tangy yogurt-based curry, enjoyed with rice or roti.
  • Laal Maas: A fiery red meat curry prepared with mutton, red chilies, and traditional spices.
  • Churma Ladoo: A sweet made of crushed wheat, jaggery, and ghee—rich and hearty.
