Haridwar - The Gateway to God

Experience the spiritual essence of India in the holy city of Haridwar.

About Haridwar

Haridwar, located in the northern state of Uttarakhand, is one of the seven holiest places in Hinduism. The city’s name translates to "Gateway to God," and it is known for its sacred temples, ghats, and the Ganges River. Haridwar is a significant pilgrimage destination, where millions of devotees visit to bathe in the holy waters of the Ganges, believing it will cleanse them of their sins.

The city is famous for the evening Ganga Aarti at Har Ki Pauri, a spectacular religious ritual that takes place on the banks of the Ganges. Haridwar is also home to several ancient temples and ashrams, making it a serene place for meditation and spiritual growth. The city offers visitors a chance to experience traditional Hindu culture, rituals, and festivities throughout the year.

Top 4 Tourist Spots in Haridwar

  • Har Ki Pauri

    Har Ki Pauri is the most famous ghat in Haridwar, known for its evening Ganga Aarti. It is believed to be the spot where Lord Vishnu left his footprints, making it a sacred site for Hindus.

  • Chandi Devi Temple

    Located on the top of Neel Parvat, the Chandi Devi Temple is dedicated to Goddess Chandi. The temple offers a panoramic view of Haridwar and is accessible via a cable car ride.

  • Maya Devi Temple

    One of the Siddha Peethas, the Maya Devi Temple is dedicated to Goddess Maya, the presiding deity of Haridwar. The temple is revered by devotees who visit it to seek blessings and spiritual guidance.

  • Shanti Kunj

    Shanti Kunj is a spiritual ashram that serves as a center for meditation, yoga, and spiritual awakening. It is known for its peaceful surroundings and is an ideal place for those seeking inner peace.

  • Rajaji National Park

    Located on the outskirts of Haridwar, Rajaji National Park is home to a variety of wildlife, including tigers, elephants, and leopards. It is a popular spot for nature lovers and wildlife enthusiasts.

What Haridwar is Famous For

  • Alsi Paratha: A unique paratha made with flax seeds, this is a traditional and healthy snack that’s commonly served in Haridwar.
  • Chole Bhature: A popular North Indian dish, Chole Bhature consists of spicy chickpeas served with deep-fried bread, a delicious and filling meal loved by locals and tourists alike.
  • Aloo Puri: A breakfast favorite in Haridwar, Aloo Puri is a combo of deep-fried flatbread (puri) and a spicy potato curry. It’s a must-try when visiting the city.
  • Rasgulla: Known for its soft texture and syrupy sweetness, Rasgulla is a popular sweet in Haridwar, often enjoyed by tourists and pilgrims alike.
  • Ganga Jal: Haridwar is famous for its holy water, Ganga Jal, which is collected from the river and often taken home by pilgrims as a spiritual offering.
