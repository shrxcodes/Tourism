Amritsar - The Holy City

Discover the spiritual and cultural richness of Amritsar, home to the Golden Temple.

About Amritsar

Amritsar, located in the state of Punjab, India, is one of the most significant spiritual and cultural hubs in the country. The city is best known for being the home of the Golden Temple, also known as Harmandir Sahib, the holiest site in Sikhism. The city’s history is steeped in the stories of Sikhism’s founding and has been a place of immense significance for centuries.

Amritsar is also known for its rich history, vibrant culture, and deep religious significance. The city's grand architecture, the peaceful atmosphere around the Golden Temple, and the colorful markets make it an iconic destination for pilgrims and tourists alike.

Top 4 Tourist Spots in Amritsar

  • Golden Temple

    The Golden Temple is the most famous attraction in Amritsar and is one of the holiest places in Sikhism. The temple is known for its stunning architecture, surrounded by a sacred pool, and is visited by millions of devotees from around the world.

  • Jallianwala Bagh

    Jallianwala Bagh is a historical garden that commemorates the massacre that took place in 1919. The site holds significant emotional and historical importance for India and is a must-visit for history enthusiasts.

  • Wagah Border

    The Wagah Border is where the flag-lowering ceremony takes place between India and Pakistan. The daily ceremony is an incredible display of patriotism, discipline, and military precision, making it a popular tourist attraction.

  • Maharaja Ranjit Singh Panorama

    The Maharaja Ranjit Singh Panorama is a museum dedicated to the life of Maharaja Ranjit Singh, the founder of the Sikh Empire. It features exhibits and models depicting the life and reign of the great leader.

What Amritsar is Famous For

  • Amritsari Kulcha: A popular stuffed flatbread that is baked in a tandoor and served with chole (chickpeas) and a dollop of butter. It's a must-try local delicacy.
  • Langar Prasad: Offered at the Golden Temple, Langar is a free meal served to all visitors, and it consists of daal (lentils), roti (flatbread), and vegetables. The experience of having Langar is spiritual and an integral part of the visit.
  • Amritsari Fish: A spicy, batter-fried fish delicacy that is marinated with a blend of aromatic spices and deep-fried until crispy. It's a favorite street food in Amritsar.
  • Chole Bhature: A hearty meal consisting of spicy chickpeas (chole) paired with deep-fried bread (bhature). It’s a filling and flavorful dish that locals swear by.
  • Lassi: A sweet, chilled yogurt-based drink served in a traditional glass, lassi in Amritsar is rich and creamy, often topped with a dollop of butter or cream.
