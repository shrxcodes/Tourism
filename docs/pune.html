About Pune

Pune, often referred to as the "Oxford of the East," is a vibrant city in Maharashtra known for its rich cultural heritage, educational institutions, and pleasant climate. It was once the seat of the Peshwa rulers and boasts a deep connection to India's history and modern-day tech landscape.

Today, Pune blends old-world charm with contemporary trends, offering visitors a variety of experiences from historical monuments to modern-day shopping hubs.

Top 4 Tourist Spots in Pune

  • Shaniwar Wada

    Built in the 18th century, Shaniwar Wada is a historical fortification and the seat of the Peshwa rulers, offering insights into Pune's rich history.

  • Aga Khan Palace

    A historical palace known for its architectural beauty, it played a significant role in India's freedom struggle, housing several important leaders during the Quit India Movement.

  • Saras Baug

    A beautiful garden and temple complex dedicated to Lord Ganesha, Saras Baug is one of the most popular spots for picnics and family outings in Pune.

  • Osho Ashram

    Located in Koregaon Park, the Osho Ashram is a spiritual retreat that draws visitors from around the world seeking peace, meditation, and relaxation.

What Food Items to Try in Pune

  • Pune Biryani: A deliciously spiced biryani, a blend of flavors influenced by the city's Mughal heritage.
  • Vada Pav: A popular street food consisting of a spicy potato fritter served in a bun, often referred to as the "Indian burger."
  • Misal Pav: A spicy curry made with sprouted beans, topped with farsan (crispy snacks) and served with pav (bread).
  • Puran Poli: A sweet flatbread filled with jaggery and lentils, often served during festivals.
  • Sabudana Khichdi: A popular breakfast or snack dish made from sago pearls, peanuts, and potatoes.
