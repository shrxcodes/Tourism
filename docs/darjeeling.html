Darjeeling - The Queen of Hills

Explore the misty hills, tea gardens, and scenic beauty of Darjeeling.

About Darjeeling

Darjeeling, located in West Bengal, is a picturesque hill station nestled in the foothills of the Eastern Himalayas. Famous for its lush tea gardens, colonial charm, and stunning views of the Kanchenjunga, it is often referred to as the "Queen of Hills."

Known for the iconic Darjeeling Himalayan Railway (a UNESCO World Heritage Site), the town is a cultural blend of Tibetan, Nepali, and Bengali influences. Darjeeling is also a hub for adventure lovers, trekkers, and nature enthusiasts looking to escape into tranquil surroundings.

Top 4 Tourist Spots in Darjeeling

  • Tiger Hill

    Famous for its breathtaking sunrise views over Mount Kanchenjunga and the distant Mount Everest on clear days.

  • Darjeeling Himalayan Railway

    A narrow-gauge railway known for scenic toy train rides through hills, tunnels, and tea estates.

  • Batasia Loop

    A spiral railway track with a war memorial and panoramic views of the Himalayas.

  • Peace Pagoda

    A Japanese temple and stupa promoting peace and spirituality, offering a calm retreat with mountain views.

What Food Items to Try

  • Darjeeling Tea: World-famous aromatic tea grown in local estates, available in black, green, and white varieties.
  • Momos: Steamed Tibetan dumplings filled with vegetables or meat, served with spicy chutney.
  • Thukpa: A hearty noodle soup with vegetables and meat, perfect for the chilly weather.
  • Sel Roti: A traditional Nepali rice-based fried bread, mildly sweet and crispy.
  • Churpee: A local cheese made from yak milk, chewy and unique to the Himalayan region.
