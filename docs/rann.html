Rann of Kutch - The White Desert of India

Explore the magical salt marshes, vibrant culture, and starry skies of Gujarat's iconic Rann of Kutch.

About Rann of Kutch

The Rann of Kutch, located in Gujarat, is a vast salt marsh and one of the most surreal landscapes in India. Famous for its stark white expanse and the vibrant Rann Utsav, the region is a cultural and natural marvel.

During full moons, the salt desert glows under moonlight, creating a once-in-a-lifetime view. It also hosts traditional handicrafts, camel rides, and desert camping experiences.

Top 4 Tourist Spots in Rann of Kutch

  • White Desert (Great Rann)

    The vast, white salt plains stretching endlessly—best experienced during sunset and moonlit nights.

  • Kalo Dungar (Black Hill)

    The highest point in Kutch offering panoramic views of the Rann and hosting the Dattatreya Temple.

  • Mandvi Beach

    A serene beach town near Rann, known for its clean sands, wind farms, and the historic Vijay Vilas Palace.

  • Bhuj

    The gateway city to Rann with historical forts, palaces, and a rich handicraft and textile tradition.

What Food Items to Try in the Kutch Region

  • Dabeli: A spicy, sweet, and tangy street food made with mashed potato mixture stuffed in a bun and topped with sev and chutney.
  • Kutchi Khichdi: A comforting dish of rice and lentils served with ghee and kadhi (yogurt curry).
  • Ghaat: A thick porridge-like preparation made with wheat and jaggery, often eaten during festivals and winters.
  • Fafda & Jalebi: A crunchy snack paired with syrupy sweets—popular in Kutch and across Gujarat.
  • Chhas (Buttermilk): A cool, spiced drink made from yogurt—essential to beat the desert heat.
