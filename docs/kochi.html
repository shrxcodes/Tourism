Kochi - The Queen of the Arabian Sea

Discover the beauty of Kochi, a coastal gem known for its rich heritage, scenic backwaters, and vibrant culture.

About Kochi

Kochi, located in the southwestern part of India in Kerala, is a vibrant port city that has long been a center of trade and commerce. Known as the "Queen of the Arabian Sea," Kochi boasts a unique blend of modernity and tradition, with its charming streets, colonial-era buildings, and beautiful backwaters.

The city's rich cultural heritage reflects its diverse history, having been influenced by Portuguese, Dutch, and British colonialism. The iconic Chinese fishing nets and the peaceful backwaters make Kochi a must-visit destination for travelers seeking both relaxation and adventure.

  • Fort Kochi

    Fort Kochi is a historic neighborhood with Dutch, Portuguese, and British colonial influences. It is famous for its quaint streets, heritage buildings, and vibrant art galleries.

  • Mattancherry Palace

    Also known as the Dutch Palace, this stunning building is a beautiful mix of Kerala and European architectural styles. It houses a museum showcasing royal artifacts.

  • Vypin Island

    Located just off the coast of Kochi, Vypin Island is famous for its serene beaches, beautiful churches, and the St. Mary's Church, one of the oldest European churches in India.

What Food Items to Try in Kochi

  • Karimeen Pollichathu: A signature dish of Kerala, this is a spicy and tangy fish (pearl spot) marinated with local spices and wrapped in banana leaves.
  • Sadya: A traditional Kerala feast served on a banana leaf, featuring a variety of vegetarian dishes like sambar, avial, and payasam.
  • Appam and Stew: A fermented rice pancake served with a flavorful stew made with coconut milk, vegetables, and meat.
  • Chemmeen Curry: A spicy and tangy shrimp curry made with coconut milk and a variety of local spices.
  • Puttu and Kadala Curry: A breakfast favorite, puttu is steamed rice cake served with a spicy black chickpea curry.
