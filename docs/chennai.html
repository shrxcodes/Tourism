About Chennai

Chennai, the capital of Tamil Nadu, is one of the oldest cities in India. It is a vibrant hub for culture, education, art, and modern technology. Known for its deep connection to classical music, dance, and architecture, Chennai is home to ancient temples, historic landmarks, and a beautiful coastline.

With its rich colonial history, diverse culture, and world-class culinary experiences, Chennai offers an eclectic mix of old and new.

Top 4 Tourist Spots in Chennai

  • Marina Beach

    One of the longest urban beaches in the world, Marina Beach is a popular spot for morning walks, evening strolls, and enjoying the coastal atmosphere.

  • Kapaleeshwarar Temple

    This ancient temple, dedicated to Lord Shiva, is renowned for its stunning Dravidian architecture and vibrant rituals.

  • Fort St. George

    Built by the British East India Company, Fort St. George is one of Chennai's historical landmarks and houses a museum showcasing artifacts from the colonial era.

  • Government Museum

    Located in Egmore, this museum is home to an impressive collection of artifacts, including sculptures, paintings, and archaeological finds from South India.

What Food Items to Try in Chennai

  • Idli: A soft steamed rice cake, often served with sambar (lentil soup) and chutney, perfect for a light breakfast.
  • Dosa: A crispy, thin pancake made from fermented rice and lentil batter, commonly served with sambar and chutney.
  • Chettinad Cuisine: Known for its aromatic spices, dishes like Chettinad Chicken Curry and Chettinad Biryani are a must-try.
