About Marina Beach

Marina Beach, located along the eastern coastline of Chennai in Tamil Nadu, is one of the longest urban beaches in the world, stretching over 13 kilometers. It is one of the most popular destinations in Chennai, known for its lively atmosphere, scenic views, and rich history. The beach is lined with iconic landmarks and statues, making it a cultural and recreational hub for both locals and tourists.

Marina is not only a place for leisurely walks but also a destination to enjoy street food, relax with family and friends, and witness breathtaking sunrises and sunsets. It is also home to some important historic monuments and attracts thousands of visitors each year.

Top 4 Tourist Spots at Marina Beach

  • Santhome Cathedral Basilica

    This beautiful church, located near Marina Beach, is one of the oldest and most famous Catholic churches in India, known for its neo-Gothic architecture.

  • Victory War Memorial

    A war memorial dedicated to the soldiers who lost their lives during World War I, located at the southern end of Marina Beach.

  • Anna Memorial

    A memorial dedicated to the former Chief Minister of Tamil Nadu, C. N. Annadurai, located near Marina Beach, with an impressive architectural design.

  • Labor Statue

    Located near the beach, the Labor Statue is an iconic landmark that represents the working class of the country, symbolizing the importance of workers in society.

What Food Items to Try at Marina Beach

  • Jalebi: A sweet and crispy dessert served with syrup, a popular treat at the beach stalls.
  • Roasted Corn: Grilled corn on the cob, a popular snack enjoyed by beachgoers, often seasoned with chili powder and salt.
  • Chaats: From Pani Puri to Bhel Puri, enjoy these famous street snacks that are perfect for a quick bite.
  • Ice Cream: Chilled treats, especially in the summer, are a favorite among tourists at the beach.
  • Fresh Coconut Water: A refreshing drink, perfect for hydration, served directly from coconuts along the shore.
