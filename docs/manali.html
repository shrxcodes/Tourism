About Manali

Manali, nestled in the Kullu Valley of Himachal Pradesh, is a charming hill station surrounded by majestic mountains and lush pine forests. It's a favorite destination for honeymooners, backpackers, and adventure enthusiasts.

Manali is also the gateway to the famous Rohtang Pass and the scenic Leh-Manali Highway. With its cool climate and scenic landscapes, it remains a top pick for mountain lovers.

Top 4 Tourist Spots in Manali

  • Solang Valley

    Known for adventure sports like paragliding, skiing, and snowmobiling surrounded by alpine beauty.

  • Rohtang Pass

    A high mountain pass offering stunning views, snow activities, and a gateway to Lahaul and Spiti.

  • Hidimba Devi Temple

    An ancient wooden temple surrounded by cedar forests, dedicated to Hidimba from the Mahabharata.

  • Old Manali

    A laid-back area with quaint cafes, guesthouses, and a vibrant hippie culture amidst mountain views.

What Food Items to Try

  • Sidu: A local Himachali bread made with wheat flour and yeast, typically served with ghee and dal.
  • Trout Fish: Freshwater trout prepared in local spices, popular in Kullu-Manali region.
  • Dham: A traditional festive meal with rice, dal, curd, and boor ki kari served on leaf plates.
  • Madra: A yogurt-based dish made with chickpeas or kidney beans and aromatic spices.
  • Babru: A Himachali version of a kachori filled with black gram paste, deep-fried to perfection.
